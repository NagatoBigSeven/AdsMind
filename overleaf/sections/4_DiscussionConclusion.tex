\section{Discussion}

\subsection{AdsMind's Contributions and Scope}

The results of this study point to a nuanced characterization of closed-loop LLM agents that differs from much of the prior literature.
Prior work on LLM agents for chemistry has emphasized the \textit{capability} axis: what new kinds of tasks can an LLM accomplish?
Recent studies have demonstrated LLM agents for materials discovery~\cite{zhang2026towards,zhang2026matclaw,li2026agentic}, autonomous atomistic simulation~\cite{huang2026towards,yang2026quasar}, and catalyst screening~\cite{chandrasekhar2026catalyst,mok2026reasoning}.
Our evidence suggests that for finding low-energy adsorbate configurations, the capability axis is not where closed-loop LLM reasoning wins.

\textbf{Energy-depth comparison.}
Under identical MACE-MP-0 small physics, random sampling with 20 relaxations matches AdsMind on 10/20 CMU cases and beats it on six.
Heuristic enumeration of 25--98 canonical sites exposes additional low-energy basins, but its largest raw wins require connectivity filtering.
Adsorb-Agent's multi-configuration search reaches lower energies in 14/16 comparable cases for GPT-5.4 and 12/16 for GPT-4o.

\textbf{Robustness across backends.}
These results are not artifacts of a single LLM backend.
The closed-loop reliability and backend-convergence results hold on CMU20 and OCD62 across four backends after external service failures are removed at the raw-attempt audit stage.

What closed-loop LLM reasoning contributes is along a different axis: the combined property of being \textit{reliable, compute-efficient, and interpretable} simultaneously.
Each of these attributes alone has precedents: brute-force controls can be reliable, heuristic enumeration is interpretable (canonical sites), and single-attempt LLM planning can be MACE-efficient but brittle.
What is new in AdsMind is the \textit{co-occurrence} of all three properties:
\textbf{Reliability:} 80/80 CMU20 Full successes, 245/248 OCD62 Full successes versus 222/248 for OCD62 1-Shot, and 320/320 successful CMU20 iterative-variant runs versus 73/80 successful CMU20 1-Shot runs.
\textbf{Efficiency:} 5--25$\times$ less MACE compute per single-backend run than brute-force alternatives.
\textbf{Interpretability:} Physically grounded reasoning artifacts (slip events, forbid constraints, site-mismatch fingerprints) that a practitioner can inspect, contest, and incorporate into downstream workflows~\cite{zhang2026matclaw,huang2026towards}.
This combination is a prerequisite for autonomous chemistry, where silent failure or irreproducibility in any one dimension would break the pipeline.
Reliability ensures the agent does not silently fail on edge cases; efficiency enables practical deployment with limited compute; and interpretability provides the transparency required for scientific trust and debugging.
Recent work on autonomous materials agents emphasizes similar requirements for reliability and interpretability~\cite{zhang2026towards,yang2026quasar}.



\subsection{Claims and evaluation}

This paper establishes four empirical claims through the completed CMU20 ablation matrix, the 1240-attempt OCD62 ablation matrix across four backends, overlap12 reproducibility repeats, and matched baseline controls.
We use the completed 20-case CMU matrix and the OCD62 matrix as mechanism tests, and use additional controls---CMU20 random placement, MACE-MP-0 large sensitivity, OCD62 overlap12 reproducibility, and Adsorb-Agent single-configuration stress testing---to define the operating envelope:

\begin{description}
    \\item[C$_1$ (reliability):] AdsMind Full achieves 100\% search success on completed CMU20 runs (80/80) and 98.8\% search success on four-backend OCD62 runs (245/248).
    \begin{itemize}
        \item CMU20: All 320 case--backend runs succeed for iterative variants (Full, $-$Slip, $-$Forbid, $-$Term), versus 73/80 for 1-Shot.
        \item OCD62: Full succeeds on 245/248 runs, $-$Slip on 247/248, $-$Forbid on 246/248, $-$Term on 247/248, and 1-Shot on 222/248.
        \item Adsorb-Agent: Single-pass runs succeed on 73--80\% of cases; single-configuration stress test produces no output on 6/20 cases.
    \end{itemize}
    These shortfalls are natural dissociation/reaction outcomes, not external service failures.

    \item[C$_2$ (backend convergence):] The iterative architecture reduces energy variance across LLM backends.
    \begin{itemize}
        \item CMU20: Mean four-backend range is 0.153~eV for Full versus 0.359~eV for 1-Shot (2.3$\times$ reduction).
        \item OCD62: Mean four-backend range is 0.183~eV for Full versus 0.316~eV for 1-Shot across cases with at least two successful backends.
    \end{itemize}

    \item[C$_3$ (compute efficiency):] A single AdsMind Full run uses $\sim$4 MACE-MP relaxations per case.
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Comparators: 20 (random), 25--98 (heuristic), $\sim$21 (Adsorb-Agent).
        \item CMU20: AdsMind matches or beats random placement on 14/20 cases under 0.01~eV tolerance.
        \item Heuristic enumeration: 11 ties, 1 AdsMind-lower, 3 heuristic-lower (5 rows unresolved).
    \end{itemize}

    \item[C$_4$ (interpretability):] Closed-loop reasoning produces structured artifacts:
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Slip events, forbid constraints, site-mismatch fingerprints.
        \item One-shot slip rates correlate with surface composition complexity across all four backends.
        \item Chemical Slip serves as a standalone evaluation signal for LLM science applications.
    \end{itemize}
\end{description}


\subsection{Chemical Slip as a standalone LLM evaluation signal}

Chemical Slip---the mismatch between an LLM-planned adsorption site and the site realized after physical relaxation---was introduced as a feedback signal for the closed-loop agent.
The slip statistics aggregated across the CMU20 and OCD62 one-shot benchmarks reveal a secondary value: slip frequency, stratified by surface type and adsorbate, serves as a physically grounded proxy for LLM chemical reasoning quality that is independent of any downstream task metric~\cite{alfred2026data,zhang2026towards}.
On the CMU benchmark (see the Chemical Slip diagnostic section in the SI), one-shot slip rates range from 20\% (monometallic) to 77\% (intermetallic) across all four backends, indicating that LLM chemical priors are substantially less reliable on complex alloy compositions under this relaxation protocol.
In contrast to text-based chemistry benchmarks such as ChemBench~\cite{mirza2024chembench}, which test chemistry knowledge and reasoning in language form, Chemical Slip evaluates \textit{applied} reasoning---whether the model's structural prediction survives physical simulation---and complements existing LLM evaluation frameworks for scientific domains~\cite{li2026agentic,huang2026towards}.
This makes Chemical Slip a general-purpose evaluation signal for LLM scientific agents: it quantifies the gap between linguistic chemical knowledge and physically realizable structural predictions.

\subsection{Why brute-force sampling wins on depth, and when it matters}

The energy-depth advantage of random placement and heuristic enumeration on complex surfaces requires explicit mechanistic interpretation.
On simple adsorbates with few competitive binding sites (H on Cu$_3$Ag(111), OH on Pt(100)), AdsMind Full matches the non-LLM baselines within 0.01~eV; the PES has few distinct basins and iterative refinement from any initial guess converges to the same low-energy basin.
On complex adsorbates (CH$_2$CH$_2$OH, OCHCH$_3$, ONN(CH$_3$)$_2$), the PES has many local minima separated by non-trivial barriers; iterative refinement from a single trajectory becomes trapped in whichever basin the first relaxation descends into, regardless of whether a deeper basin exists elsewhere.
Broad initial sampling circumvents this trap by seeding multiple trajectories in different basins~\cite{lan2023adsorbml,zitnick2020introduction}.

This observation matters practically because it delineates when closed-loop search is the right tool.
For high-throughput catalyst screening where thousands of candidate surfaces must be down-selected and abundant parallel compute is available, brute-force methods can be more wall-clock efficient (all relaxations run in parallel) and their failure modes are tolerable (failed cases can be re-queued), even though they consume $5$--$25\times$ more MACE-MP relaxations per case than AdsMind.
For autonomous exploration of a single promising candidate where failure is costly and interpretability is required, closed-loop refinement adds value.
A two-stage pipeline---brute-force screening followed by closed-loop refinement on top candidates---would combine the throughput of the former with the reliability of the latter; the concrete architectural integration is sketched at the end of the Conclusion.
Recent work on multi-agent materials discovery emphasizes similar hybrid approaches~\cite{zhang2026towards}.

\subsection{MLFF as surrogate: scope and validation}

All primary benchmark and matched-baseline experiments in this study use MACE-MP-0 small as the physical backend~\cite{batatia2023foundation,deng2023chgnet}.
The agent's value is orthogonal to MLFF accuracy: it optimizes search strategy, not the energy function itself.
Recent work has demonstrated the integration of MLFFs with LLM agents for materials discovery~\cite{zhang2026matclaw}.
Adsorption energy in this study is operationally defined within the fixed MACE-MP-0 small protocol: all ablation comparisons, Wilcoxon tests, and backend-convergence metrics are evaluated on energies produced by the same locked MACE-MP-0 small calculator, so within-protocol rankings between configurations on the same surface are self-consistent by construction.
We do not claim that MACE-MP-0 small rankings match DFT rankings in absolute terms; rather, the MACE-MP-0 large run described below serves as a sensitivity boundary that quantifies how far absolute energies shift under a higher-fidelity force field, without being treated as ground-truth validation.

To characterize this sensitivity, we repeated the GPT-5.4 AdsMind Full variant on all 20 CMU cases with MACE-MP-0 large (GPU, float64, dispersion enabled), and on a 10-case OCD62 sensitivity slice.
MACE-MP-0 large shifts absolute AdsMind energies substantially: on CMU20 the mean absolute shift relative to MACE-MP-0 small is 1.255~eV, or 0.834~eV when the anomalous case~20 is excluded.
The direction of the shift is case-dependent, with 15/20 CMU cases becoming more stable and 5/20 becoming less stable under MACE-MP-0 large.
Case~20 is an anomaly rather than a clean failure: the summary retains a non-dissociated best structure at $-2.402$~eV, while later dissociated analyses reach lower unretained energies.
On the OCD62 sensitivity set, MACE-MP-0 large succeeds on 9/10 cases, with case~043 failing naturally through slip and dissociation.
Because Adsorb-Agent was not rerun with MACE-MP-0 large, this experiment should be interpreted as a force-field sensitivity check on AdsMind trajectories, not as a second AdsMind--Adsorb-Agent head-to-head.
Representative DFT validation remains necessary before interpreting the MLFF-ranked structures as final adsorption energetics beyond the fixed surrogate protocol.

Known MLFF failure modes---dispersion-dominated binding, strongly correlated $f$-electron systems, strong charge-transfer interfaces---define the current scope boundary of the framework.
Extending to these regimes will require either higher-fidelity MLFFs or hybrid MLFF--DFT workflows, but does not require changes to the agent architecture.

\subsection{Limitations and negative results}

We organize the limitations into four categories: algorithmic, coverage, statistical, and scope.

\paragraph{Algorithmic limitations.}
\textbf{Energy depth on complex adsorbates.}
On cases with multiple competitive binding sites (16, 17, 18, 19), AdsMind Full produces higher energies than broader-sampling baselines.
A five-seed control reduces but does not remove this gap: the best-of-seeds AdsMind result remains 0.90~eV higher on case~16 and 1.20~eV higher on case~17.
The mechanism is the iterative trajectory's tendency to stay within the initial energy basin.
We regard this not as a defect but as a specification of the operating envelope.

\textbf{FORBID over-pruning.}
Removing the FORBID constraint \textit{improves} the final energy on several cases (GPT-5.4: BH $p = 0.084$, $r = -0.79$).
This indicates that explicit negative constraints over-prune the search space on surfaces where a lower-energy site was initially rejected.
Adaptive or soft-constraint variants merit future investigation.

\paragraph{Coverage limitation: configuration-space sampling.}
Exhaustive control experiments reveal that on complex intermetallic surfaces with multiple competitive binding sites, the dominant factor in finding low-energy configurations is often simply \textit{the number of distinct initial configurations sampled}.
Random placement (20 relaxations) finds materially lower energies on 6/20 cases; heuristic enumeration (25--98 sites) wins on 3/20 validated cases; and Adsorb-Agent ($\sim$21 relaxations per case) reaches lower energies on 14/16 comparable GPT-5.4 cases and 12/16 GPT-4o cases.
These results make clear that broader sampling matches or exceeds closed-loop refinement at the cost of 5--25$\times$ more MACE-MP compute.
The question is not which system finds the absolute lowest energy---an LLM agent cannot realistically out-sample a brute-force enumerator with sufficient budget---but rather: \textit{what is the contribution of closed-loop LLM reasoning when we hold compute, physics, and data fixed?}

\paragraph{Statistical limitations.}
Although the CMU and OCD-GMAE benchmarks are large enough for Friedman tests to reach significance ($p < 0.01$ for all comparisons), many pairwise Wilcoxon tests do not survive BH correction.
The OCD62 matrix introduces natural failures, so we report failure-aware success rates and success-conditioned energy deltas instead of forcing ordinary paired tests.
We interpret comparisons primarily through effect sizes and bootstrap confidence intervals, relying on the consistent direction of effects across backends as conceptual replication.

\paragraph{Scope and reproducibility.}
\textbf{Scope.}
The current implementation handles flat and stepped crystalline surfaces with a single adsorbate species.
Extensions to defects, nanoparticles, coadsorption, and solvation are architecturally straightforward but require additional validation~\cite{yang2026quasar,zhang2026towards}.

\textbf{LLM reproducibility.}
Hosted LLM services are black-box systems subject to unannounced model updates.
We mitigate this by recording configured model identifiers and frozen experiment settings, and by releasing structured result files and analysis scripts.
The multi-backend design ensures that conclusions do not depend on any single LLM backend~\cite{alfred2026data,huang2026towards}.


\section{Conclusion}

We have presented AdsMind, a closed-loop multi-agent framework that integrates LLM-driven planning with iterative MLFF relaxation feedback for autonomous adsorption configuration search.
Three mechanisms---Chemical Slip detection, FORBID constraint memory, and autonomous termination---enable the agent to detect and correct its own reasoning errors.

\textbf{Experimental validation.}
Through the completed CMU20 ablation matrix, the 1240-attempt OCD62 ablation matrix, overlap12 reproducibility repeats, and head-to-head comparisons against non-LLM and LLM baselines, we establish that AdsMind's primary value lies in four properties:
(i)~reliability (80/80 CMU20 Full successes vs. 73/80 for 1-Shot);
(ii)~backend convergence (2.3$\times$ reduction in energy spread on CMU20);
(iii)~compute efficiency (5--25$\times$ fewer MACE-MP relaxations than brute-force controls);
(iv)~interpretability via slip events and forbid constraints.

\textbf{Negative results.}
We report that on complex adsorbates, brute-force sampling reaches lower energies than closed-loop refinement.
This trade-off arises from single-trajectory iteration rather than insufficient compute, and motivates future hybrid architectures.
Chemical Slip additionally serves as a physically grounded diagnostic for LLM chemical reasoning quality.

\textbf{Broader implications.}
Closed-loop LLM reasoning is not a replacement for brute-force search on well-resourced problems; rather, it is the foundation for \textit{autonomous chemistry workflows} where reliability, efficiency, and interpretability must be achieved simultaneously.
A natural next step is to combine AdsMind's error recovery with broader initial site enumeration, yielding an agent that inherits the depth of brute-force methods and the reliability of closed-loop refinement.

\textbf{Future directions.}
Extending AdsMind to handle co-adsorption, solvation effects, and defected surfaces will require minimal architectural changes but significant validation.
The Chemical Slip diagnostic could be adapted to evaluate other LLM-based scientific agents, providing a general framework for assessing applied reasoning in physical domains.
A two-stage pipeline---brute-force screening followed by closed-loop refinement on top candidates---would combine the throughput of broad sampling with the reliability and interpretability of iterative reasoning, representing a promising direction for production deployment.
