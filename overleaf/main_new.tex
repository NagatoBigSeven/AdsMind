\begin{abstract}
Identifying the most stable adsorbate--surface configuration is central to heterogeneous catalysis and electrochemical energy conversion, yet remains computationally expensive and expert-intensive.
While machine learning force fields (MLFFs) have dramatically reduced per-evaluation cost, the upstream \textit{search strategy} problem---deciding which configurations to evaluate---remains unsolved.
Existing LLM-based agents for this task operate in an open-loop, single-pass regime that cannot recover from incorrect site predictions.
This work introduces \textbf{AdsMind} (\textbf{Ads}orption with \textbf{M}achine \textbf{I}ntelligence \textbf{\&} Fee\textbf{d}back), the first closed-loop multi-agent framework that enables autonomous error correction through MLFF relaxation feedback.
Three mechanisms implement this closed loop: \textit{Chemical Slip} detection diagnoses mismatches between planned and realized coordination; \textit{FORBID} constraints prevent retry of failed site families; and convergence-based early stopping avoids unnecessary computation.
Evaluated on 1,640 matched ablation attempts across CMU20 and OCD-GMAE62 using four LLM backends (Gemini~2.5~Pro, Grok-4, GPT-5.4, Claude~Sonnet~4.6), AdsMind achieves 100\% Full-variant reliability on CMU20 and 98.8\% failure-aware reliability on OCD-GMAE62, where the remaining Full failures are natural reaction/dissociation outcomes rather than API or calculator errors.
It also reduces cross-backend energy variance by 2.3$\times$ on CMU20 and consumes 5--25$\times$ fewer MLFF relaxations than brute-force baselines.
Notably, we report the negative result that broader initial sampling outperforms closed-loop refinement on energy depth for complex adsorbates, a trade-off that clarifies the operating envelope of LLM-driven reasoning.
Chemical Slip further provides a physically grounded evaluation signal for LLM chemical reasoning quality.
By delivering reliability, efficiency, and interpretability simultaneously, AdsMind establishes a foundation for autonomous chemistry workflows.
\end{abstract}
